Bonus Strategy

Still the easiest edge many users ignore

Bonuses and free bets are not just promotions — they are bankroll tools. Used correctly, they reduce risk and stretch strategy over time.

  • Open multiple accounts instead of using only one bookmaker
  • Track rollover rules, odds restrictions, and expiry dates
  • Treat bonus funds like real money and protect your bankroll
  • Smaller up-and-coming books often offer better bonuses than giant brands

Value Betting

The most important long-term principle

A value bet exists when your probability estimate is higher than the probability implied by the bookmaker’s odds.

Example: odds 2.50 imply 40%. If your analysis says the real chance is 50%, that is value.

  • Compare your own number with the market price
  • Use daily predictions as a value-hunting starting point
  • Stay careful with paid systems that overpromise

Bankroll Management

Protect the machine first

Good bankroll management prevents one bad run from destroying long-term progress.

  • Only bet money you can afford to lose
  • Use fixed or adjustable percentages of bankroll
  • Record bets, odds, outcomes, and profit/loss
  • Let discipline control emotion

Fibonacci & Martingale

Powerful, but dangerous if used blindly

Both systems can recover losses in theory, but both can also create very large stakes if variance turns against you.

  • Fibonacci grows slower than Martingale
  • Martingale doubles after each loss
  • Both require strict stop-loss limits
  • Bookmaker max limits can break the sequence
